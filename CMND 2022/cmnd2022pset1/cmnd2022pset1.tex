    Suppose that $I\sub J$ and let $P\in V(J)$. Then $f(P) = 0$ for all $f\in J$, whence again we have that $f(P) = 0$ for all $f\in I$. \\

    As $a^1\in I$ for all $a\in I$, it is clear that $I\sub \sqrt{I}$. Hence, by the previous part $V(\sqrt{I})\sub V(I)$. Now suppose that $P\in V(I)$ and let $f\in \sqrt{I}$. Then there exists some $n\in \N$ such that $f^n \in I$, so $(f(P))^n = 0$ whence $f(P) = 0$ as $k$ is a field (and thus an integral domain).\\
